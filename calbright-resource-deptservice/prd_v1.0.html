1. Background & Problem

Current State

Calbright College serves adult learners — roughly 70% work full- or part-time while studying — and a large share of them face real financial pressure (single-parent households, hourly wages, high cost of living). Today the Student Portal's Resources page lists external relief agencies as static cards (Legal Assistance, Mental Health, Tax Credits, Voter Resources, etc.). Students must identify which programs apply to them, self-assess eligibility, and contact each agency individually.

Meanwhile, actual financial relief for this population is distributed across 40+ government and non-profit programs — tax credits (CalEITC, YCTC), utility discounts (PG&E CARE, LIHEAP), county emergency assistance (Fresno County Emergency Rental), federal student loan IDR plans, food assistance, and more. Each has different eligibility rules, different applications, different timelines.

Our reference student — Mariah Villalobos, 32, single mother in Fresno, Medical Coding program, ~$28,470 annual income — likely leaves at least $4,940 in annual relief unclaimed simply because discovery and qualification are too hard.

Core Problems

  • Fragmented relief programs — 40+ programs across multiple websites with no unified entry point for students
  • No eligibility guidance — students must self-assess qualification against complex rules (AGI thresholds, family size tables, county residency, filing status), creating heavy cognitive load
  • Privacy anxiety — disclosing financial hardship to a Student Success Coach feels like surrendering dignity; many students would rather go without help than have their income seen by staff
  • Money left behind — eligible students miss an estimated $4,000+ in annual relief because no system nudges them toward it

Why Now

  • The Resources page already exists in the Student Portal — this is a surface-level upgrade, not a new product
  • Staff Portal (Career Partnership Hub) has an established card-based design system — the coach-facing view reuses existing components
  • Rules-based eligibility matching is tractable today for the top ~10 programs; ML is not required for v1
  • GLBA + CCPA compliance framing provides a clean architectural constraint ("staff cannot see financial data") that doubles as a student-trust feature
  • Shared localStorage contract between Student Portal and Staff Portal enables a demonstrable cross-portal flow without backend work for the prototype

2. Goals & Metrics

Primary Goals

  • Reduce time-to-match — shrink the path from "I don't know where to apply" to "here is your personalized list" to under 3 minutes
  • Protect student dignity — financial details never visible to coach (GLBA + CCPA compliant by design, not just by policy)
  • Close the loop without coercion — coach sees the student took action, and can offer follow-up only when the student explicitly opts in

Success Metrics (6 months post-launch)

Non-Goals (v1)

  • Not a case-management system — no approval flows, no ticketing, no case notes
  • Not a financial counseling platform — the engine surfaces matches; it does not negotiate with agencies or advise
  • Not a submission intermediary — students still apply on the external agency's website; this tool only routes them
  • Not a replacement for the existing Resources page cards (Legal, Mental Health, Voter, etc.) — this is a new addition, not a rebuild

5. Detailed Design

5.1 Student Portal — Resources Page Card

Location

Inserted in the existing Resources page card list, in the third position (after Legal Assistance and Mental Health, before Tax Credits).

Two-State Interaction

  • Landing (State A) — single-column full-width card with illustration on the left, title + description paragraph on the right. Hover adds an accent left border (#D34508) and subtle shadow.
  • Expanded (State B) — on click, the layout splits into a 40% / 60% grid. Left rail holds compact cards (illustration + title only, description hidden); right panel renders a "booklet" with a short explainer, 3 value bullets, and the primary CTA.
  • Clicking the active card in State B returns to State A (no-op removed in favor of toggle).
  • Left rail scrolls independently within a fixed 800px height; right panel scrolls but hides the scrollbar.
  • On mobile (< 768px), State B collapses the left rail and shows only the booklet.

5.2 3-Step Intake Modal

Step 1 — Basic Information

FieldTypeRequired
Household sizeCustom single-select (1 / 2 / 3 / 4 / 5+)Yes
Children under 6Radio (Yes / No)Yes
Employment statusCustom single-select (Full-time / Part-time / Not employed / Self-employed)Yes
Areas you need help withMulti-checkbox (Tax / Utility / Rent / Emergency / Student loans)No

Dropdowns use a custom component — not native <select> — so mobile rendering is consistent and matches the brand accent on open. Validation on "Continue" shows an error toast in red (#D34508) when any required field is missing.

Step 2 — Upload Documents

  • Two slots: Income Verification Letter (PDF), 2024 Tax Return / Form 1040 (PDF)
  • "Try with sample document" button for each slot auto-fills demo filenames for stakeholder walkthroughs
  • Uploaded state: borderless light-green background, flat SVG check icon, filename + size, 28px × close button top-right to clear the slot
  • "Continue" disabled until both slots are filled

Step 3 — Review & Consent

  • Read-only summary of Step 1 answers and uploaded file names
  • Privacy notice (navy left border) explaining CCPA + GLBA scope
  • Two consent checkboxes, both required:
    1. "I consent to automated processing of my documents for eligibility matching"
    2. "I understand my coach will see my submission status but not my financial details"
  • "Submit & See My Match" disabled until both checkboxes are checked

5.3 Loading State + Results Page

Loading (~4 seconds)

Engine-working animation with 3 bouncing accent dots, a progress bar (accent → yellow gradient), and rotating status lines that communicate the sensitive-data pipeline:

  1. "Decrypting your documents..."
  2. "Reading income and tax data..."
  3. "Matching against 47 support programs..."
  4. "Calculating your eligibility..."
  5. "Preparing your personalized recommendations..."

On completion, the shared CalbrightState.markActionTaken(matchCount) is called — persisting state to localStorage for the Staff Portal to observe.

Results Header

Light gray background (not navy — intentionally reduces stimulation when the user has just submitted sensitive data), navy title, green dollar total, gray subtitle:

  • Title: "You may be eligible for 4 Relief Programs"
  • Amount: $4,940+ (green, 32px)
  • Subtitle: "Estimated total annual benefit · Based on your income, family size, and location"

Program Card Anatomy

  • Category tag (TAX CREDIT / UTILITY / EMERGENCY) + match strength tag (HIGH MATCH / MEDIUM MATCH)
  • Program name + one-line description
  • Green benefit amount
  • Why-you-qualify bar (light-green background, green check)
  • Actions: See required documents (expands docs list) · Call enrollment line (expands phone info) · Apply online (accent-filled, opens real external URL in new tab)

Bottom Bar

  • "Email these to me" — triggers top-right slide-in toast
  • "Talk to Rondy about this" (accent-filled) — on click: flips to confirmed state (check icon + "Conversation requested" text, disabled), triggers CalbrightState.markSupportRequested(), shows toast "Rondy will see your request and reach out within 1 business day"

5.4 Staff Portal — Financial Hardship Card

Location

First card in the right column of the student detail view, above Career Partnership, Referrals, and Leave of Absence. Reuses the existing .card / .card-header-light / .card-body / .appt-row classes — no new CSS.

Three States

StateStatus TextIconVisual TreatmentVisible Rows
noneN/AnoneDefaultStatus only
action_takenAction TakenLock (flat SVG)Navy 3px left border, navy status text, "Details →" linkStatus · Submitted · Programs Matched
support_requestedSupport RequestedBell (flat SVG)Red 3px left border, red status text, "Details →" linkStatus · Submitted · Programs Matched

Icon swapping is done via pre-rendered inline SVGs with display toggles — no innerHTML mutations (DOM-safe per the write-time security checks).

State Sync Contract

The Staff Portal registers an onStateChange listener against window.CalbrightState. When the Student Portal writes action_taken or support_requested via localStorage, the storage event fires cross-tab, the listener runs, and the card re-renders with the new status, timestamp, match count, and border color.

5.5 Staff Portal — Financial Hardship Details Modal

Opened by tapping "Details →" on the card. Layout and three color-coded information panels make the privacy model legible at a glance:

  • ✓ What you can see (green left border, dark-green header) — submission timestamp, programs matched count, follow-up flag. All non-financial metadata.
  • ✕ What you cannot see (red left border, dark-red header) — income details, uploaded W-2 / tax return contents, specific program recommendations. Explicit negative space is a feature, not an oversight.
  • Why? (navy left border) — short paragraph citing GLBA Safeguards Rule and CCPA as the legal rationale; frames it as "deliberate data minimization", not "system limitation".

Only in support_requested state, a fourth panel appears — Suggested Next Steps — with three full-width buttons: Schedule a 1:1, Send a follow-up message, Share additional resources.

The top-right close button is the shared .modal-close-x (red #D34508 ×) — applied consistently across all 12 Staff Portal modals for a unified close affordance.

5.6 Eligibility Engine Rules (v1 prototype)

Rules are declarative and client-side for the prototype. For the v1.0 launch, rules move server-side behind an audited API — the contract stays the same. Each rule is a condition predicate plus an output shape:

program: CalEITC
condition: agi < 31950 AND state == "CA" AND filing_status in [single, hoh, mfj]
output:
  benefit_estimate: computeFromAGI(agi, 2024_caleitc_schedule)
  match_score: high
  why_qualify: "AGI of ${agi} is below the $31,950 threshold"

9. Roadmap

Phase 0 — Demo Prototype (April 2026, current)

  • Static HTML/CSS/JS, client-side eligibility rules, 4 hardcoded matches for Mariah's demo data
  • Cross-portal state sync via shared localStorage contract (shared/state.js)
  • For portfolio showcase + internal stakeholder alignment

Phase 1 — MVP Launch (Target Q3 2026)

  • Real backend (encrypted document storage, audit logging)
  • Server-side rules engine with the 4 anchor programs: CalEITC, YCTC, PG&E CARE, Fresno County Emergency Rental
  • SSO integration with Calbright identity provider
  • Consent + revocation endpoints; deletion flow
  • Staff beta with 5–8 Success Coaches

Phase 2 — Scale (Q4 2026)

  • Expand to 20+ programs: federal student loan IDR plans, CA state aid, CalFresh / food assistance, LIHEAP, county-level emergency funds
  • Spanish language support (UI + engine outputs)
  • Coach-facing aggregated insights dashboard (anonymized counts, dollar totals, trend charts)

Phase 3 — Intelligence (Q1–Q2 2027)

  • ML-assisted matching improvements (learn from outcome data)
  • Outcome tracking: did the student actually enroll in the recommended program? Close the loop with external APIs where available (e.g., FTB)
  • Policy admin UI — non-engineers can update rule thresholds as laws change (no engineering deploy required)

Compliance References

  • GLBA Safeguards Rule (16 CFR Part 314) — financial data handling standards
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — right-to-know, right-to-delete for California residents
  • Why not FERPA? FERPA covers educational records. Financial PII collected for eligibility matching falls under GLBA. Using the right legal framing unlocked the correct data minimization architecture — coach sees status only, engine sees documents, audit log sees everything.

Design System Notes

  • Palette: cream #FFF7E8, navy #001E5A, accent #D34508, yellow #F5BB11, green #1A7A4A
  • Font: Lato (300 / 400 / 700 / 900)
  • Icon system: inline flat-stroke SVGs (Feather style) — no emoji in production UI
  • Toasts: top-right slide-in, white background, 4px accent left bar, 3.2s duration; error variant uses accent text color
  • All modals share the red .modal-close-x close affordance

Out of Scope (v1)

  • Case management workflow for coaches (approval flows, case notes, status pipelines)
  • Direct filing integration with external agencies (IRS, FTB, PG&E) — students still apply on the external site
  • Peer support or community features
  • Financial literacy curriculum integration
  • Tax preparation services (student uses a separate service and returns here for relief matching)
